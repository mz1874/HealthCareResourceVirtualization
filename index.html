Hospital Distribution Overview (2010-2020)

The hierarchical bar chart shows the distribution of hospitals by ownership and category from 2010 to 2020. The data includes general, public, and private hospitals (for-profit and not-for-profit).

Hospital Ownership Models:

  • Public Hospitals: Government-funded and focused on essential services, often in underserved areas.
  • Private Hospitals: Divided into:
    • For-Profit: Revenue-focused, offering specialized services.
    • Not-for-Profit: Run by charitable entities, reinvesting surplus into services.

General Hospitals:

General hospitals cater to a wide range of healthcare needs and may be publicly or privately operated, serving as the backbone of the healthcare system for communities of all sizes.

Healthcare Policies (2010-2020):

Government initiatives and private healthcare regulations during this period likely influenced hospital distribution and types, such as funding for public hospitals or incentives for private ones.

Healthcare Hierarchical Distribution Treemap

This interactive treemap visualizes the hierarchical structure of healthcare services and their relative values. Each rectangle represents a category or sub-category within the healthcare system, such as departments, organizations, or types of services. The size of each rectangle corresponds to its value, which could indicate budget allocation, number of facilities, patient volume, or other relevant metrics.

How to Read the Treemap

  • Size of the Rectangles: The larger the rectangle, the higher the value or importance of that category within the hierarchy.
  • Color Coding: The rectangles are color-coded to help distinguish between parent categories and their children.

Interactivity

  • Zoom In: Click on a rectangle to zoom in and explore its sub-categories. This allows you to drill down into specific details within each section.
  • Zoom Out: Click outside the rectangle or on the top-most rectangle to zoom out and return to the previous level.
  • Breadcrumb Navigation: The path displayed at the top helps you keep track of where you are within the hierarchy.
